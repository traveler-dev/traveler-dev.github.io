The Statistics

20.000 Cameroonians were killed by road accidents between 2010-2015; more than malaria and AIDS combined. A Cameroonian is ten times more likely to be killed by reckless driving than Boko Haram in the North of the country. To worsen things, emergency services hardly get notified about an accidents, so victims suffer\die. Road accidents are not just limited to Cameroon; according to WHO, 1.24 million people die as a result of traffic accidents and up to 50 million are injured every year with 90% of victims occurring in developing countries which account for less than 47% of registered vehicles.

Our Solution


Convinced? Just

'Traveler' is a mobile phone app which monitors the performance, speed, location and number of passengers on a bus, and with the help of 'big data' and 'machine intelligence' alerts drivers and authorities to potential dangers. The system automatically sends high speed alerts each time a given bus runs above regulatory speed limits. With an integrated cloud service and a 'back-end' monitoring system, we can provide 'predictive analysis' and recommendations to road safety officials which can help reduce road accidents. In the unfortunate case of an accident, the app detects its location automatically and notifies emergency services, hospitals and families of all users for free. The app uses internet, but is designed to work both On and Offline. Online, we do real time tracking and analysis of performance and incidents(accident detection, high speed alert). Offline, the app works as a black box device, tracking a driver's performance and incidents while saving them locally (inaccessible to anyone l). When internet connectivity becomes available, the app synchronizes with the server. We also have the first integrated offline emergency assistance guide to passengers. This would empower users of our app and give them the tools they need to deal with emergency situations at the "last mile" generally inaccessible to emergency services. Traveler is the first startup in Africa to leverage the power of big data, cloud computing, and machine intelligence\learning to predict and prevent road accidents.
